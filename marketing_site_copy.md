## Problem Section

### Heading

Provider dashboards are not enough.

### Body

Provider billing views are useful, but they are not the same thing as operational truth.

Teams shipping real AI products run into the same problems quickly:

- historical APIs are inconsistent
- some providers need admin-level keys for useful history
- app usage is hard to attribute to projects, teams, or workflows
- spend shows up after the fact instead of where decisions happen

Tokenometer solves that by measuring the app traffic itself, then using provider history as reconciliation where it exists.

## How It Works

### Heading

Three ways usage enters Tokenometer.

### Intro

Live metering is the primary path. Provider history and CSV backfill are supporting paths.

### Block 1

#### Title

Live metering

#### Body

Your app sends requests through Tokenometer, or reports usage back after a direct provider call. This is the strongest and most immediate source of truth.

### Block 2

#### Title

Provider reconciliation

#### Body

When providers expose historical usage, Tokenometer imports it and compares it against live totals. That helps detect drift and improve confidence.

### Block 3

#### Title

Manual backfill

#### Body

CSV and export imports help bring older usage into the system without pretending manual backfill is the same thing as live traffic.

## Differentiation

### Heading

Why Tokenometer is different.

### Points

#### 1. Live metering first

Tokenometer treats live app traffic as the main truth source instead of depending only on provider billing history.

#### 2. Integration identity

Apps become named, owned integrations with health, environment, project, and team context.

#### 3. Metering-path transparency

Every event can say whether it was proxy-captured, signed-ingest, provider-sync, CSV import, or estimated.

#### 4. Reconciliation in context

Tokenometer does not hide provider-history mismatches. It surfaces them directly where spend is being reviewed.

#### 5. Governance on top of usage

Usage becomes operational, attributable, and governable instead of just visible.
